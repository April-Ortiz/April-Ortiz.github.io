Twitch Tutorial

Follow the steps below for a tutorial on how to sign up for your first Twitch account!

Twitch, next to YouTube, is one of the most widely known streaming services available at this time. It is a free platform that allows a user to create an account and watch people play games. If you’ve ever wanted to try a game before you buy it, now you can, sort of… Twitch has many different types of games, genres, and categories that you can choose from.

They range from “Just Chatting” which is solely the creator talking to their chat; all the way to the latest “Call of Duty” where you can watch someone play the campaign or just public matches. Once you create your Twitch account, you can also set it up to stream your own “Just Chatting” or specific game. Now, you can learn how to do both!

  • Click "Sign Up" in the top right
  • Choose your username (make it good as you can only change it every 90 days!
  • Fill in your Password, Date of Birth and Email address
  • That's it! You've just signed up for your very own Twitch account!

What do you do once you have your Twitch account?

Now that you have your account set up, click your icon in the top right of the page. This will bring down a list of things you can do with your new account

From here, click on settings. This will allow you to set up your channel! Going through each tab will allow you to set up your channel preferences, security and privacy, notifications, connections and recommendations.

Clicking on "Channel and Videos" will take you to another page where you will find this menu on your left side. From here, you can really personalize your channel. While on the "Channel" page, you can add your bio, social media links if you have them and even add a streaming schedule!

Clicking "Moderation" on the left, will allow you to add "chat rules" to your channel so anyone who watches your stream can see the rules you want them to follow. You can also block certain terms or phrases as well as set up an auto mod to do the work for you.

Clicking "Stream" on the left will allow you to see your Stream Key which you will in order to stream from OBS.

Finally, from the "Stream Manger" option on the left, you can see your dashboard. This will be what you can use when you stream in order to moderate chat, see your playback as well as any notifications that happen during your stream. This is important so you can interact with your viewers!
